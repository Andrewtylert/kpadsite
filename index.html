Uninsured Kansans in 2023

In 2023, a total of 239,807 Kansans were living without health insurance. This includes individuals from all age groups across the state.

Breakdown by Age Group

Age Group Uninsured Count
Children (under 18) 38,534
Adults 196,446
Seniors 4,827

Poverty and Medicaid Expansion Impact

Of the uninsured population, 43,993 Kansans are currently living below the federal poverty level. If Medicaid were expanded in Kansas, approximately 38,088 of those individuals would become eligible for coverage.

Expanding Medicaid could provide critical access to healthcare for thousands of low-income Kansans, helping reduce long-term health disparities and improving economic stability for vulnerable families.
